Frequently Asked Questions

When should students start career counseling?

Ideally, career counseling should begin in 9th grade when students start making academic choices that impact their future. Early exposure to career options helps students make informed decisions about streams, subjects, and extracurricular activities that align with their goals.

How do we assess a student's aptitude?

We use a combination of standardized aptitude tests, interest inventories, personality assessments, and one-on-one counseling sessions. Our holistic approach evaluates cognitive abilities, learning styles, personality traits, and genuine interests to provide comprehensive guidance.

What's the difference between aptitude and interest?

Aptitude refers to a student's natural abilities and potential to develop skills in specific areas, while interest indicates what they enjoy doing. The ideal career path combines both - something the student is good at and passionate about. We help students find this sweet spot through careful assessment.

How often should students revisit career guidance?

Career guidance should be an ongoing process. We recommend formal check-ins at key transition points: after 10th grade, during 12th grade, at college entry, and before graduation. However, students can access our counselors anytime as interests and goals evolve.
